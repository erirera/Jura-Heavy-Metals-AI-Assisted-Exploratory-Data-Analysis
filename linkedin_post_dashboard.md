🚀 **What happens when AI meets Geospatial Data?** 🌍

I recently took the classic **Swiss Jura heavy metals dataset** (359 geo-referenced samples) and built an end-to-end data pipeline to lay the groundwork for some exciting AI-assisted environmental research. 

Instead of staring at static spreadsheets, I developed a fully interactive **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) Dashboard** (pure HTML/Chart.js) that brings the geology to life. 

Here is what I built:
🔍 **Dynamic Visualizations**: Instant access to frequency histograms, Pearson correlation heatmaps, and customizable metal-pair scatter plots.
🗺️ **Spatial & Categorical Context**: Visualizing the distribution of 7 heavy metals (Cd, Cu, Pb, Co, Cr, Ni, Zn) across different coordinates, rock types, and land uses.
✂️ **Machine Learning Ready**: Pre-split the dataset into a strict training (n=259) and validation (n=100) set for robust predictive modeling.

**Why does this matter?** 
This is the perfect proving ground for testing autonomous **AI Agents** in the environmental sciences. Whether it's multi-output spatial interpolation (like AI-augmented Kriging or Graph Neural Networks) or autonomous pollution anomaly detection—this project bridges the gap between raw geochemical surveys and actionable, AI-driven insights. 

The ultimate goal? Building AI agents that can rapidly analyze complex spatial data, train predictive models, and spot environmental hotspots faster than ever before.
